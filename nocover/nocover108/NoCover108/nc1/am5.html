Amiga 5000 ?

Normalerweise wollen wir ja keine Texte aus der Dfü veröffentlichen. Das soll eine absolute Ausnahme sein. Und da wir keine Texte verändern ist hier mal die englische Version:

I found this text in the latest magazine in Sweden: Dator Magazinet #19 1993



Now it have been comfirmed! The Amiga 5000 will be a multiple-platform computer which can run both Windows NT and the operativsystem of Mac. It will appaer in summer 1994. This was revealed by Commodore's Executive Director in GB David Pleasance.

Rumours and speculations about Commodore's new Amiga model have been circulated for a time in most foreing computer-magazines. But Commodore has (as allways) denied this, and refused to give any information what so ever. But now David Pleasance, known for "leaking" secret informations, opened his mouth. I a interview by the english magazine Amiga Format he reveals the essence of A5000.

Accordingly to DP it's a totally new step in the development of the Amiga. Commodore have discarded the thought of making it backwards compatible (so software for the A5000 can not run on any elder Amiga). Instead they put all their effort in creating a RISC based Amiga, which should be able to compete with all other platforms, based on the RISC and Pentium processors. The A5000 will also be able to run Windows NT and thereby PC applications that works with it. But on a A5000 the programs will run 5 times faster than on a Pentium PC!

Theoretically we could put in Mac chips and run Mac-programs, says David Pleasance. We aim on a computer that's open for anything. Amiga 5000 will also have brand new graphics-chips called Trippel A which have 10 times higher performance than today's AGA chips that comes in the A1200 & A4000. A5000 exists only as a prototype at the moment, but it will be released in the summer of 1994. It won't be a computer designed for the privat home. The price will probably be high, somewhere around the price for a Pentium PC.

On the long term we will use the technology in the usual Amiga's too.
