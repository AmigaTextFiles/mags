2. AMIGA, Inc. reagiert auf Artikel in MacWeek

AMIGA, Inc. hat sich vor kurzem mit einem offenen Brief an die AMIGA-
Anwender gewandt, in dem angebliche technische Details des neuen AMIGA-
Systems, die das Macintosh-Magazin MacWeek veröffentlicht hat, als 
vollkommen falsch zurückgewiesen werden:


»An Open Letter to the Amiga Community - October 29, 1998

The recent article in MacWeek concerning the Gateway/Amiga computer is
completely false.

While entertaining reading there is no truth to the story.

I am sure that we will all look forward to the next story from another non-
Amiga publication, as they try to get it right.

Thank you again for your support.

Bill McEwen
Head of Marketing, Amiga Inc.
billm@amiga.com
425-413-2620
Fax 425-413-2640«

In dem betreffenden Artikel der MacWeek hieß es beispielsweise, der neue 
AMIGA solle mit einem G3-PPC-Prozessor ausgeliefert werden und einen 
Macintosh-Emulator enthalten.


In einem Newsgroup-Posting spielt Fleecy Moss im selben Zusammenhang auf 
den neuen Consumer-Rechner von Apple, den iMac, an:

»Thu, 29 Oct 1998 08:12:36
comp.sys.amiga.misc
Mac the knife? Mac the Naff more like...
fleecy@netreach.net
fleecy moss at Black Sheep Enterprises

For those concerned about the recent Mac the Naff article describing 
LazyRus, the new snoozy walrus rumoured to be breeding up in the flat, flat 
lands of South Dakota, please rest assured that this story is about as 
factual as a Milosevic peace accord.

We would never present the Amiga community with something so ordinary.

Whilst Apple and all their little pips may be happy with an average machine
stored in a translucent blue potty, we are set to rewrite the manifesto for 
the digital information revolution.

...unfortunately, as with most good things, it takes time.«
