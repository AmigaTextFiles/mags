1. Geheimnis um Hersteller des 'MMC' offenbar gelüftet

Das Rätsel um den Hersteller des in Amiga-Kreisen 'MMC' genannten
Mediaprozessors, der in der nächsten Amiga-Generation verwendet werden
soll, scheint gelöst: Unter Berufung auf Amiga International hat ein User
in einem öffentlichen Amiga-Forum gemeldet, dass die Firma kürzlich von
dem kanadischen Grafikhardware-Hersteller ATI aufgekauft worden sei.
Unter der Voraussetzung, dass dies der Wahrheit entspricht, kann es sich
bei dem Hersteller des 'MMC' wohl nur um die Firma Chromatic Research
(http://www.chromatic.com) handeln, die ATI im November erworben hatte.
Bereits vor Monaten war Chromatic Research in Gerüchten als möglicher
Hersteller des 'MMC' genannt worden. Nähere Informationen zu dessen 
Mediaprozessor MPact sind auf http://www.mpact.com verfügbar.
Nicht ganz klar ist, ob Amiga, Inc. auch nach dem Aufkauf von ATI auf die
Technologie von Chromatic Research setzen wird. Zumindest wird man sich 
wohl nicht starr darauf festlegen, sondern das neue Amiga-Betriebssystem 
möglichst Hardware-unabhängig entwickeln.

Hier drei Presseerklärungen zum Aufkauf von Chromatic Research durch ATI:

» ATI Technologies Inc. in Talks with Chromatic Research Inc.

Toronto, Canada, October 8, 1998 - ATI Technologies Inc. (TSE:ATY) the 
world leader in 3D graphics/video acceleration and multimedia solutions 
today announced that it is in negotiations regarding a possible acquisition 
of Chromatic Research, Inc. At this time, no agreement has been reached or
entered into and there can be no assurance that the transaction will
proceed. Further terms will be announced if an agreement is reached.

Chromatic is engaged in the development of media processors from its
facilities in Sunnyvale, California. This technology would enhance ATI's
graphics/multimedia portfolio and augment the Company's position in the sub
$500 PC, set-top box and consumer appliance industries.

Founded in 1985, ATI employs more than 1,300 people at headquarters in
Thornhill, Ontario, and in offices in the United States, Germany, France,
the U.K., Ireland, Barbados, Malaysia, Taiwan and Japan. ATI is a public
company whose shares trade on the Toronto Stock Exchange. Visit our web

» ATI Technologies Inc. Agrees to Acquire Chromatic Research, Inc. Core
 technology for System-on-a-Chip designs used in sub $500 Computers,
 Set-Top Boxes and Consumer Devices

Toronto, Canada, October 21, 1998 - ATI Technologies Inc. (TSE:ATY) the
world leader in 3D graphics/video acceleration and multimedia solutions,
today announced it has entered into a definitive agreement to acquire
Chromatic Research, Inc. for a total investment of US$67 million, comprised
of 100% of the outstanding shares of Chromatic and assumption of
Chromatic's net liabilities.

The purchase is consistent with ATI's long history of integrating core
technology blocks and responding to key market inflection points. Chromatic
is currently developing next-generation System-on-a-Chip (SOC) technology,
a highly integrated product that encompasses graphics, system logic and
processor blocks into a single chip. Chromatic's next-generation technology
is an ideal candidate for numerous multimedia applications incorporated in
sub-$500 computers, set-top boxes as well as other consumer electronic
devices.

"This move is intended to extend our lead in the graphics and multimedia
industry", said KY Ho, ATI's president and CEO. "Chromatic has a history of
developing excellent technology. ATI has the infrastructure and economies
of scale to enable success in this new product category."

"Chromatic's development to date adds significantly to our technology
portfolio and allows us to extend our competencies into fast growing
markets," said Adrian Hartog, ATI's vice-president, engineering. "This
higher level of integration allows us to address fast growing segments in
the sub $500 computer, set-top box and consumer electronics industries."

ATI will be acquiring all in-process research and development related to
future products of Chromatic, equal to more than 300 man-years of both
hardware and software engineering, all rights to their intellectual
property including a significant patent portfolio relating to over 100
inventions and fixed assets. Chromatic currently employs approximately 140
engineers. The purchase of Chromatic will be financed through cash. The
transaction is expected to close, subject to Chromatic's shareholder and
the necessary regulatory approvals, on or about November 13, 1998.

About Chromatic
Founded in 1993, Chromatic Research develops media processor technology for
use in low cost PCs, set-top boxes, and consumer electronics products. The
company is privately held with headquarters in Sunnyvale, California.

About ATI 
ATI Technologies Inc., the world's largest supplier of 3D graphics and
multimedia technology, designs, manufactures and markets innovative and
award-winning multimedia solutions and graphics components for personal
computers. An ISO 9002 company, ATI is the world's leading supplier of
video and 2D/3D graphics accelerators to OEM and retail customers. Founded
in l985, ATI employs more than 1,300 people at headquarters in Thornhill,
Ontario, and in offices in the United States, Germany, France, the United
Kingdom, Ireland, Barbados, Malaysia, Hong Kong and Japan. ATI is a public
company whose shares trade on the Toronto Stock Exchange.
