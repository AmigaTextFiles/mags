My Amiga


I got a C64 when I was in junior high, and after using it solely for gameplay I got an A500. This was really good, but after getting my first 52 Mb harddrive I was caught. Upgrading to an A600 with a 40Mb harddrive and filling it was a chore, but then I got this monster at 1.2 Gb, and I have never ever filled anything of that since.

Lacking in power put not prestige I had to retire it (it is now resting in a box in the basement along with with Vic 20 I picked up for about $4 at a yardsale) and got an A1200. Unfortunately I burned out the PAR-port on that one (my bad!) and bought another. This was back in 1997.

After moving I came in contact with another Amigan; Stig, and with his help and guidance I upgraded to a towerbox, a 060-CPU and lots of other neat stuff, this after my external squirrel-SCI CD-player had a nice... well, let's say fire-related accident.

This machine is now used for everything; text- and picture-editing, surfing, email, word-processing and layout-work. It performs splendidly even in its currently crippled state (see below) and only at 50 MhZ it is still on par (work-wise) with any Wintel-computer I have ever worked with. I also make homepages with this machine, and still all by hand.


Amiga Specs

  • A1200 (since 1997. I had a C64, A500 and A600 before that)
  • 68060 @ 50 MhZ
  • 64 MBbytes of RAM
  • Harddirve (used to have two, CD and CDRW but I had a fire in the IDE-port and now I can only run the one HD... A pity)
  • 56k Modem
  • Serial I/O-blix, external soundport, external floppy, and such

I am Member of the "I am Amiga"-club since three days after it opened, and ordered my A1 in the very beginning of November.
