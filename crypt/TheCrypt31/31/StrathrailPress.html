PRESS RELEASE No 44Date: 21.7.03
Subject: Class 31, 31327 for the Strathspey

Agreement has been reached between the Strathspey Railway Company and the owner of the final Brush Class 31, No. 31327 (D5862), George Littlejohn, for the use of the locomotive on the Speyside line. The engine, which has been overhauled at Bo'ness, arrived on the line on 16th July.

Although not a native to the Highlands the loco will be a useful addition to the diesel fleet replacing as it does D8020 which has returned to Bo'ness. The loco's duties started almost immediately by hauling an early morning special working for Pathfinder Tours on Saturday 19th July to and from Broomhill. Its normal duties will include working "The Royal Scotsman" on its visits to the railway and occasional turns on the Strathspey's regular services between Aviemore, Boat of Garten and Broomhill.

It is thought that the first appearance on timetabled workings will be on 4th August. (Check the Superintendents page on the railway's web-site:


http://www.strathspeyrailway.co.uk


At time of purchase No. 31327 was located at Carlisle Upperby having been in store since 1996 and was moved from there by road on 8th November 2001 to Bo'ness where restoration to full working order was undertaken by the SRPS Diesel Group under the leadership of Stuart Seller Jr.

Now in Grey Railfreight, Red Stripe livery the loco was trialled successfully at Diesel Gala days on the Bo'ness & Kinneil during October '02 and May this year.
