THE CRYPT 22

A Very Big welcome to a Very Big edition.

Yes, folks this edition is our largest yet filling up 7 floppy disks and using up some five and a half megabytes of crunched data. We have 96 articles for you to read and 126 Pictures for you to view. Amiga users also have three background music modules to listen to.

How did we get to do so much in such a short space of time? Well it was bloody hard work I can tell you, but we didn't do it alone. We had the help of the finest and hardest working group of men and women an Editor could ever hope to work with. I am of course referring to The Team that make up RIYAN Productions.

My thanks go out to each and everyone of you at RIYAN who's hard work, determination and dedication makes this magazine what it is, the best read on the internet.

But we at RIYAN can't take all the credit our selves, because YOU the reader played a huge part too. Just scan through our pages and see just how many articles and pictures you the reader submitted. Again an Editors dream come true. My thanks go out to each and everyone of you as well.

Now Ian has already mentioned the changes within RIYAN Productions so I won't repeat the information all over again. However I would like to wish Steve Hyde and Craig Daines well in their new ventures and of course welcome newcomers Kathy Danby and Bruce Steers into the fold. With the re-organisation within RIYAN I know that we can all look forward to new and exciting things in future editions.

One of the most exciting changes that I am looking forward to is the creation of the next edition. As Ian explained this is the very last time we will be using DMC (Disk Magazine Creator) Farewell old friend you have served us well. HTML a strange new beast that I know so little about will takes its place.

What will this "foreign" language do for the CRYPT? Well it will make the magazine truly compatible on both the Amiga and PC platforms. We will no longer be restricted to low resolution screen displays of just 16 colours and Ian says it's dead easy to use!!

We can have pictures embedded within the text and articles can be linked to others within the magazine if we should require to do so. On line with the new version will link you to other sites too. Wow, can't do any of that with the old DMC program.

For me I see it as a challenge, as I will now no longer as Dual Editor be restricted to an old disk based program. OK so I have a new language to learn but I'm sure I will master it. What it will mean is that don't have to write two versions of the magazine anymore, (Amiga disk and hard drive) that alone will free up extra time to spend on the imput of the magazine. I look forward to it very much.

You will find many new features in this edition. Crypt Cuisine, Amiga On-line and North of the Border being just some of them. No I won't tell you about them here, you will have to read them yourself.

Finally I hope that you all enjoy this edition as much as we all enjoyed putting it all together for you. Go on what are you waiting for .... Read the CRYPT 22

Ray C. Hawkins (aka The Dungeon Master)
