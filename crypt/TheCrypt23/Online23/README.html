For The Floppy Disk Users.

In the past The Crypt was created on floppy disks and converted to run from hard disk on its completion. This was very difficult and also very time consuming! Imagine having to decide from day one of starting a new edition how many disks would be required when all you had was perhaps a couple of loading graphics and maybe a few articles? A crystal ball would have been very useful to us!

Times have changed now and there are very few floppy disk users left. The Crypt is therefore now compiled on hard drive and copied to floppy as we build up the magazine. This is much easier and faster for us at RIYAN Productions and the time saved is spent in bringing you a better and bigger edition.

Will The Crypt Still Run From Floppy Disk?

Yes, because your reading it now. However as you continue your way around this edition you will soon see the main differences. There will be more and more prompts for you to insert a different disk. Obviously if you had several floppy drives the disk changes would be less frequent. In theory if The Crypt came on 5 disks and you have 5 disk drives you wouldn't have any trouble at all!

So How Do I Transfer The Crypt to Hard Drive?

Easy folks..

1) Create a drawer somewhere on your hard drive and name it The Crypt (number).

2) Now copy ALL the contents of DISK 1 to it.

3) Next in that drawer make two new drawers and name them PICS and TEXT.

4) Look at DISK 2 and apart from the disk icon you will see two drawers, PICS and TEXT. Copy all the files in PICS drawer into the PICS drawer on your hard drive.

5) Now do the same for the TEXT files.

6) Now do exactly the same for the rest of The Crypt floppy disks.

7) Job complete. If you followed these simple instructions correctly The Crypt will run from hard disk. There are no assigns to worry about as the IconX script takes care of that when you double click on The Crypt Icon.

8) Enjoy YOUR magazine.
