IAN'S EDITORIAL



Welcome to Issue 23 of the New CRYPT Magazine. This of course is our first "html" Only version of the Magazine, And we certainly hope that you ... The Readers agree with us that this New look was worth waiting for.

In prior issues we at RIYAN Productions ..Produced three separate versions of the CRYPT

(a) DMC Disk Version (Amiga only)

(b) DMC HD Version (Amiga Only)

(c) html Version (Amiga/PC)

One or Two readers have expressed certain concerns over this Move, However we believe that when you browse through the Pages in this issue, That even the Doubters will agree that the CRYPT is greatly enhanced and improved by these changes.

We at RIYAN Productions would dearly love to hear any comments you may have, Regarding this New look ..So please feel free to Drop us a note (Good or Bad) Either in our Site Guestbook or via e-mail

ian@ferret22.freeserve.co.uk

This issue contains loads of interesting articles, Including Photos of the NEW AmigaOne Board .. A review of "The Feeble Files" (Amiga Game) .. A review of a TV card for your PC ..And of course more exciting News from our Friends at Vulcan Software.

Although this Issue May appear slightly Smaller than some of our Past Issues ... I can assure you that it is in fact one of our Biggest :).

Past Issues (Using the Old DMC Program) Meant that we Could not include Screenshots/Pic ..etc. In with our Articles, Thankfully this restriction has now been lifted. We believe that this Issue is more colourful and entertaining than anything we have produced in the past.

Now as you trawl though the Pages in this Issue ..You can't fail to notice that we have several Pages/Articles ...Not related to Computers ... This of course does not mean they are not of Interest to the USERS of the Computer. Indeed I have had several comments regarding various subjects that we cover (Recipes/Steam Corner etc) and all have been very supportive.

If you have any suggestion for a Topic that you would like to see covered in the Pages of the CRYPT ..Feel free to contact us, Or why not join out active (and very funny :) Mailing list.
