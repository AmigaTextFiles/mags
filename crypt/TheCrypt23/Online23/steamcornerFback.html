Flashback Item


A certain grace and style, once synonymous with the railways, has given way to a basic "get you to where you're going" ugliness.

The picture detailed below indicates much more than just a railway, and can be dated circa the turn of the last century. The station-master, resplendent in Topper and Tails (the uniform of that time) was a most important individual in the community, for it was through him that all manner of station/railway business was conducted.

Locomotives and rolling stock were immaculately presented, and railway-men took a real pride in their jobs, as is self evident in this picture. Isn't it rather sad that the present day fails to deliver the same "feeling?"
