All in a Plumber's Work


By B.L.Cann


A frantic phone call early on in the day,
Announces "Our water is going astray !"
"We'll be a short while, before we can come over,
Have you painted the walls like the white cliffs of Dover?"




We arrive shortly after to observe the scene,
A sample of panic, like none there has been,
"Oh, help!" says the lady in a soaking wet frock,
"Madam, it would help to turn off the stop-cock!"




We survey the damage with a highly trained eye,
And move to one side, as a sofa floats by,
The flow of the water is finally abated,
"It's time for a tea break" my mate quickly stated.




With crowbar and blowtorch, a repair to do,
We paddled around until we found a clue,
A nail in a wall with a large hole around,
Conclusion - the leak we have certainly found.


A compression collar is rapidly fitted,
As back and forth the housewife she flitted,
"A nice cup of tea, boys; before you depart?"
Felt like saying "No water!", but did not have the heart.
