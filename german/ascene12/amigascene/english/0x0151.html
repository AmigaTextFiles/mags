Hi!

In http://www.amigascene.com/english/0x0122.html you write "In 1986
Karsten Obarski, whose trademark was as musician with "reLine" (Oil
Imperium, Traders), found that chip tunes and sample loops really weren't
the final solution for the insight of computer musicians, and began to
develop a program on the basis of samples, which would make simple
composition of music pieces possible, in that samples were used as
instruments, with 4 channels (tracks, hence the word Tracker) available -
with this, one could really make a start. The program SoundTracker V1.0
wasn't sold in as great the numbers as the number of copies that were
around shortly thereafter - yeah, there were plenty of crackers, and why
pay when you could get it by some other means?

Soon there were umpteen versions of this program around and in the end
Karsten gave up developing the program further. Understandably, who still
has any interest when your own program creation is not sold, but instead
widely stolen and disassembled?"

But there is more to the story. It actually started earlier with a
tracker program used by Loricels (I think it was them anyway) that used a
tracker program called (I think) "Sound FX" for their games. It was just
used within their company but then someone hacked the player source and
used it to create a music player called "MegaTrax" (have you noted that
old tracker player sources use mt_ for all labels, guess where that comes
from). MegaTrax was very hard to use because you had to write the tunes
in the source. So Karsten Obarski creates an editor for MegaTrax. The
rest is history...

Regards,
Ralph 124C41+ of PowerDrive
hitch-hiker@earthling.net
