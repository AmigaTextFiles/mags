Bill McEwen Executive Update


February 3, 2000

Greetings to one and all:

A great deal has occurred over the last couple of weeks, and it is only going to get hotter as we move forward.

In the last two weeks we have announced our new OS partner, began technical training and began meetings with future technology partners.

The response has been overwhelming, and we are but keeping our heads above water.

On January 8, 2000 at CES in Las Vegas we announced our partnership with Tao-Group as our foundation OS partner. This is a strategic relationship and brings us great rewards. I have asked Fleecy to write a short document highlighting the Amiga/Tao relationship from a more technical perspective, other than my telling everyone how incredible great and fantastic it is.

In the next two weeks we will make another strategic partnership announcement with regards to the new Amiga and the operating system.

After a week long trip to Germany and England I have come back with a much better understanding of what we can not do. As mentioned before we are not going to make promises or product announcements. We will announce to you, the community, only after the deal is signed. This will help preclude from the continuance of rumors, and the continued deterioration of the Amiga.

Our job as a company is to create exciting market opportunities for Amiga developers to take advantage of, and Amiga users to enjoy. This is more than just an upgrade to the OS. This is a complete strategy involving all aspects of the business.

Many of you have sent us business proposals, and product marketing ideas, for this we thank you, and ask your patience as we look over these plans. Some of them offer us some good ideas and perspective into areas and desires of the Amiga community as a whole that we may not have considered.

Over the next several weeks there will be some companies that come out with their own plans, or state that we do not have the community at heart as we move on. The reality is, they do not know what we have planned. In order to keep focused and to keep the incredible pace up and moving we are not sharing our plans outside of our signed partnerships. This means that if you hear something that you have not read on our site, or from a member of our team directly, it is not true.

Plain and simple.

If you did not hear it from us, or read it here on this web site it is not true.

We are not asking for your trust or your undying belief. We only ask that you take us on our words and deeds.

We will begin official meetings in Germany, England and the US for developers and dealers. This will include introductory training seminars for the new OS, and introductions to the retail pricing and schedules. We will also begin the process of bringing out the new web site, and more details will emerge as this happens.

I want to thank each and everyone of you for your hospitality during our meetings and your continued opportunities for us to move forward. We have had the chance to meet with end users, developers, dealers, and new partners.

There is a great deal of activity, and some great things happening. New partners, old faces joining the team, and we are already in the process of moving into bigger facilities.

Keeping the faith,

Bill McEwen and the rest of the Amiga Team.
