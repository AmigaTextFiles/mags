Demo Available!

A couple of weeks ago I brought you the news that Sadeness Software had signed up Invictus Teams excellent looking game, OnEscapee. Well, I can now inform you that a demo of this game has been made available and should be on Aminet by the time you read this.

OnEscapee is another in the line of eagerly awaited CD-ROM releases for the Amiga. Only now are us Amiga users beginning to see the benefit of the CD-ROM medium with games like OnEscapee using them to the full.

The demo of the game itself is over 5Mb in size, this has been cut down from the original demo which was a massive 50Mb in size! One you download it you will notice why there has been so much said about this game, the graphics are excellent and the animation really has to be seen to be believed. You thought Flashback was good, well, you ain't seen nothing yet.

The charactor moves along very smoothly, and is able to perform a wide range of actions. One thing that I did notice is that there is a small pause between you moving the joystick and the charactor moving, I would expect this could make things a little tricky if the action gets hectic.

The special effects that the gamme boasts can also be seen in the demo, such things as rain (Which looks excellent) and special light effects all help to make this game a very good visual experience.

Due to the size that the demo has been cut down too, it is quite limited. It does, however, gove you a nice insight into how good this game could be. The demo consists of two scenes, the Dumping Ground and Part of the Base Section. In the full demo (which will be available on magazine CD-ROM's) there is also the Quarry Cave section and an underwater part.

Sadeness are looking to release the Amiga version around October time with the PC version to follow a couple of months later.

For those of you who own Graphics cards, the game doesn't currently support them. However, after the full game has been released a patch will be made available free of charge that will introduce support for them. The game won't really benefit from a graphics card in anyway, it's just so ECS owners with cards will be able to play the game.
