PIOS One no Mac clone (30-May-97 )

    Dr. Peter Kittel repeats once again that the ONE is no Mac clone, but a machine compliant to CHRP or PPCP. The MacOS will run on it, but it was not designed to just run the MacOS. the ONE's architecture is a mixture of the A2000/A3000 and the BeBox, plus one I/O chip designed by Apple and another I/O chip designed by Intel thrown in.
     · Posted by Dr. Peter Kittel in comp.sys.amiga.misc [L].

Colors vs. resolution (29-May-97 )

    Dr. Peter Kittel says Commodore had to chose between "more colors" or "better resolution". Due to time- and money-constraints, both couldn't be implemented in the AGA chips. The users wanted more colors, and so higher resolutions were scheduled for a later date (and eventually completely forgotten).

AmigaOS 3.1 informations (21-May-97 )

    George Noel gives precisions about the history of the AmigaOS 3.1. In 1994 Commodore were too worried about going bankrupt to put OS 3.1 in new AGA machines. Village Tronic was the only company who got the rights to produce and distribute the OS 3.1. This was the only way to get OS 3.1 in your computer, and this monopoly was the main reason for the high price tag.

Petro T. Statement (16-May-97 )

    In an official statement by Petro Tyschtschenko at a press conference in London, May 16, 1997, Petro gives a brief summary of the Amiga's recent history, looks optimistically in the future and gives information in which general direction AI will be heading. He says they will be working with developers through concepts such as the "Open AMIGA Initiative". For us to keep the market alive it is neccessary to assist many companies in developing products through broad licensing. Our licensing policy will be very open, broad and focus on licensing and standard O/S, Chipsets and the trademarks.

PIOS One "in the spirit of" Amiga (04-May-97 )

    According to Dr. Peter Kittel, the PIOS One introduces the architecture of Amiga accelerator cards into the PPCP/CHRP world. The ONE a PPCP/CHRP machine, not a PC, but provides means to use PC cards. They are using the pOS, because it follows the principles of AmigaOS of being extremely compact and efficient and because it has the potential to become the fastest OS on the PPC and on the PC, as proDAD also plan a port for Intel PC's.
