Hi,

This is the very first editorial for the What's Hot On The Amiga Magazine, an offline Amiga magazine which will bring all the major Amiga news articles along with sometimes more screenshots of games than another Amiga online magazine.

This first issue should have been out by the end of September, but various problems with putting everything together have slowed me down, resulting in this late edition.

This first issue contains a vast majority of the big news which happened in September, and so this issue is a big larger than normally would be. But the next issue will be out next week, and will become a bi-weekly for the next issue onwards.

This issue still looks a bit ruff round the edges, but the next issue will look a lot better. Now, September saw a lot of major announcements such as the announcement of Voyager NG 3.0, some of Amiga International's plans for the future of the Amiga and Nova Sector Enginnering getting a license to build Amiga compatible computers, along with loads of announcements of new Amiga games coming or just around the corner and the News Section is dominated by such articles.

Now this month's, Site Of The Month is Nova Sector Engineering, who have on their web site some impressive specifications of their new Amiga compatible computers that will be coming out soon and have a mention in this month's News In Brief and their web site is definitely one to keep watching.

In the next issue, there will be a number of articles on Phase 5's PowerPC accelerator cards and more games, along with other stuff, with the possibility of more information on Nova Sector Engineering's CorDel Alpha and CorDel Nitro Systems.

Before, I end this issue's editorial, I'm trying to get What's Hot On The Amiga Magazine on the web, so if you any free web space out there, then e-mail at:

Hope that you enjoy this issue, and will read the upcoming issues.
