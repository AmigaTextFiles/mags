Cookies (06-Mar-97 )

    Victor Ducedre corrects some mistakes concerning Cookies that were posted in reply to the IBrowse 1.1 announcements (which supports Cookies). He reminds people that Cookies are not PC or Netscape specific and that they are neither programs nor viruses. They are like a token or passport containing specific information that a server gives to a client. Cookies do not provoque more advertisements and they can't run programs on remote computers. Cookies are merely bits of information.

Educational Amiga, PC advantages and Amiga history (06-Mar-97 )

    Dave Haynie thinks that the Amiga would be perfect for a course in operating systems. It does most of the things right, in a modern way, and it doesn't tie the hands of the programmer. He adds that the good thing about PC's is that you can drive 5-10 miles to Computer City or 30 other stores and buy a PClone, which is no longer possible with the Amiga. On the other side, the Amiga system is smart enough to avoid the question, it doesn't matter how well it's supported at the computer store, since the customer is fat and happy at home, getting real work done, playing the game, and probably oblivious to the problems PC folk have. He then points out once again that the Amiga is fairly old; that the last major high-end work was done in 1989 and 1990. AGA in 1991 and 040 boards in 1992 were the last real projects, the time between then and The End was split between "polictical delays" and "stuff you'll never really see"....
     · Posted by Dave Haynie in comp.sys.amiga.misc [L].

Amiga Successors (06-Mar-97 )

    Dave Haynie philosophises about the possible Amiga successors. Only the marketplace can name the true Amiga successor. He adds that PIOS originally intended to help the new owners [of AT] to port the OS to RISC; but since that wasn't possible, they have chosen to back other OS's. pOS hold the best promise for making an instantly recognized AmigaOS environment on the PowerPC, while the BeOS is the most forward looking OS around on any CPU, and also very true to the goals of the AmigaOS, if not pre se an API-compatible implementation of those ideas. He finishes with the interesting remark (or suggestion?) that the marketplace will crown the new king, or maybe even elect a congress instead..

New PPC chip (06-Mar-97 )

    Carl Muller posts informations about a new PowerPC chip code-named "Arthur". It will start at 250 MHz and will be available by midyear. It should be 90% faster than a 603e and 40% faster than a 604e (integer speed). Other advantages are a new L2 cache interface, a smaller surface and less power consumption than Intel's Klamath and a top speed of 350 MHz.

Haynie comments, part 2371 (05-Mar-97 )

    Dave Haynie again, this time he comments on the Alpha processor and possible uses for a QuikPak machine running NT on the Alpha side of the computer (AmigaOS on the other). He then compares the market of Alpha's and PPC's and concludes that only the PPC marked is reasonable. According to Dave Haynie, the reason QuikPak's 4000T models are so expensive is that A4000 machines are inherently expensive and QuikPak can't perform miracles.
