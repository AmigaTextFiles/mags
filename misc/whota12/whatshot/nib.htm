SASG International Announces MagicWB 2.1p Released for Free Download

Following popular demand, MagicWB 2 (which was previously only available through prior registration) is now for the first time publically available as fully installable shareware version. Get it if you still have the old and outdated MagicWB 1.2p running on your system or if you haven't yet installed MagicWB...

This version is fully installable and usable, hence you can very much evaluate if you like MagicWB or not and if it's worth to register it. By installing this version you are also updating to the new MagicWB color-allocation scheme which makes you compatible with other applications using MWB. After this installation no graphics, icons etc. should appear in the wrong colors again. And the icon build-up and general workbench performance will be much faster than before:


Download MagicWB 2.1p now

What is MagicWB?

The definite WB-Enhancer no Amiga can do without. MagicWB is a full replacement for the graphic user interface of the Workbench and has become the standard on the Amiga. MagicWB 2.x is completely restructured on a new color-allocation scheme which more than doubles the speed of the WB and reduces chip-consumption to only a third compared with all previous versions and also features a highly improved color palette. MWB 2.x introduces a never-before-seen standard for over four dozen of new image-drawers to achieve a more diversified look on your WB. More icons, dock-brushes, a dozen of new patterns, fonts and a AA-support complete this special product.

Note: If you already are a registered user of MagicWB 2, don't install this shareware version but read the "About MagicWB" readme first and copy the udpated MagicWB fonts over your old fonts. That's all.

Latest From Amiga International

At the Pianeta Amiga Exhibition on 20/21 September 1997 - Empoli , Italy, Petro Tyschtschenko, that the upcoming 3.5 release of AmigaOS will be out around February 1998, and Contrary to what Darrek Lisle said at another conference, OS 3.5 will be a software and a firmware upgrade (ROMs) and also Amiga International will try to keep the cost as low as possible. Also Petro indicated that they still haven't made up their mind on which processor will be used in the next generation Amiga, with a possible prototype for a new Amiga appearing in late 1998. Petro said that more would be revealed at the Cologne Computer Show.

Aphasia

Aphasia is another new shoot-em-up which will be coming our way, and being in the very early stages of development, the game is set to be released around the beginning of 1998 at the earliest.

There aren't that many details on the game at the moment, except that it will contain probably 6 levels which will comprise of a mix of both vertical and horizontal scrolling. A very nice feature of this game, is that the ship will be able to transform into a mech, and it will also be able to change aspect depending on what weapons are being used.

The game will also boast some special light and water effects, and also 3 different languages and in-game speech will also be there. I haven't be able to get any screenshots as yet, but they will appear here as soon as I can get soon. Finally, a demo should be available sometime around Christmas.

Vulcan Selling CD-ROM Drives

Vulcan Software have announced that they will now be selling a CD-ROM drive package. The package will consist of a 4x SCSI CD-ROM and a Squirrel interface to connect it to your A600/A1200.

Vulcan who are considered to be the biggest Amiga publisher in the world have made this move on the back of announcements that nearly all of there future games will need at least a 4x CD-ROM drive as a requirement.

The whole package will cost only £149.99, this also includes delivery.

Hopefully people will who don't already have CD-ROM drives will take Vulcan up on this offer. If not you may miss out when games like Genetic Species, Hard Target and Wasted Dreams are released.

For more information please contact Paul Carrington at Paul@vul- soft.demon.co.uk.

The World Foundry Sign With Vulcan Software

A deal has just been signed between Vulcan Software and The World Foundry which means that all related products and development teams for Explorer 2260 and Maim & Mangle. Vulcan Software is now going to be Publishing both of these games.

Explorer 2260 is a 1st person 3D trade/ simulation game set in the year 2260 and Maim & Mangle is a real-time strategy game in the same style as Dune II or Command & Conquer. As revealed previously their games will have PPC versions.
