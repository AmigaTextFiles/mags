Hi,

This issue's editorial for the What's Hot On The Amiga Magazine, starts out on a bit of a bad note, as the What's Hot On The Amiga Magazine web site at Koyote is no more and this is because the offer of web space has been withdrawn due to the time constraints, but I'd like to thank, BCo for the use of their web site.

But don't worry, What's Hot On The Amiga Magazine can still be found on the web, at:

http:/www.sonic.net/~alanwall/whota/index.html.

This issue contains a survey, which I hope you'll fill in and send back to me as I was asked by Geosync Media last week, to provide information of the specifications of the Amigas that you're using currently.

This issue contains a number of articles on the Computer97 show, an article on DCE Amiga Compatibles, another article on Foundation, but there are three articles in this issue which you'll find of great interest adn they are Petro Tyschtschenko's Speech at Computer '97, FratzenGeballer, Follow-up To Trapped 2 and Index Information Ltd Announces The Immediate Availability Of The Access Computer Powered By Amiga articles, but Myst also turns up in number of articles this issue as well.

You'll notice that this issue is bit smaller than last two issues, and this is due to the lack of news, which is evident in the News Section and this issue's News In Brief. As usual, the collection of articles split more or less evenly by game news and the more serious stuff. There is only one exclusive this issue, the More MAE Pics article, which contains some more pics from MAE which didn't make it quite in time for the last issues, with many thanks going out again to Alan Crandall who sent the pics them.

The anticipated announcement of what AI's New Amigas would be like, did'nt materialise at Computer97, but more information on when OS upgrades would be appearing was welcomed.

Now this month's, Site Of The Month is Rebol, which is Carl Sassenrath's vision of what computing should be like and not the way that it is now. An alpha version is only currently available to those on the E-mail list. Keep an eye on this site.

Check out Links for the latest additions to my Links Section.
