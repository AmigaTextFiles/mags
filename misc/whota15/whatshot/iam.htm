Intangible Assets Slashes Prices on Key Products

FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE 19-Nov-97

TWO BIG BOOSTS FOR THE AMIGA!

Every Amiga owner needs a utility to bannish the dreaded "Not Validated" and "Checksum Error" requesters. As well, every Amiga owner should be connected to the Internet. Former Commodore-Amiga Software Engineer Dale L. Larson is doing something now to bring these both tools to more of the Amiga community, and he's asking for your help.

Intangible Assets Manufacturing, Larson's company, is immediately slashing 50% off the list price of it's two most popular and widely praised Amiga products, DiskSalv4 and the book "Connect Your Amiga! A Guide to the Internet, LANs, and BBSs". The new prices are US$20 and US$12 respectively.

Even if you are already connected to the Internet, the book provides valuable background and reference information, as well as information on local networking and other communications topics.

DiskSalv4, by Dave Haynie, is simply the premier Amiga disk utility, a must-have for every Amiga owner.

Asked how he could afford to offer these reductions, Larson replied "I can't afford not to. The Amiga community is in a difficult place while we all wait for new technology, and this year has been difficult for my company financially in the Amiga market. There is a method to my madness."

"DiskSalv is a utility that should be a part of the OS, and connection to the Internet is a vital part of modern computing that is easy and cheap on other platforms. Getting this stuff out there for every single Amiga is necessary to the very survival of the Amiga. By making my products so inexpensive, I hope to give the Amiga a needed boost, and I hope to make up the difference by selling a much larger quantity. At this price, no Amiga user has an excuse not to own these great products. It's a win-win situation for IAM and for the community."

Larson added, "Please pass this press release along to your friends, user groups and others. (User Groups: Contact me for details or see www.iam.com/amiga/groups.html for even lower pricing for bulk orders made in the next six weeks.)
