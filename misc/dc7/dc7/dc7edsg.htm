I read with sadness in the Amiga section of Computer Shopper, that Fred Fish has finally decided to hang up his fishing rod, and cease releasing Amiga software. He started in 1986, releasing disks of good quality Amiga Shareware & Public Domain software, concentrating on quality, more than quantity. His software collection soon grew, as more and more programmers sent him their masterpieces, hoping to be included in his next batch of releases. He then concentrated on making CD's with the software on it instead of floppy disks, as the user would get a much better deal for there money.

It was not long before other people were emulating their releases, and trying to simulate the benchmark that Fred had already established.

It is a pity to see him go from the Amiga world, as he has brought a great many quality programs to an even greater audience (myself included). A great figure who will be sadly missed. As for how long it takes him to achieve the same results in the PC world - well we can only wait and see.

I am getting slowly addicted to talking to people on Yahoo Chat. For those who do not know what that is, let me explain. Basically, it is like a CB radio, except you know who is listening to you conversation, and you can talk to as many people as are in your room. The chat site is best described as a massive hotel. Every room in the hotel is a different conversation, and to change conversations, you just move to another room. Now there can be hundreds of people in one room - or conversation, which makes it too crowded. For this reason, duplicate rooms are created if it gets too crowded.

Everyone who is chatting uses an id or nickname to describe themselves. My nickname is Stickyspanner, due to the work I do with cars etc. I have seen some pretty sad names as well like Icantgettosleepwiththishardon, or sexy_bitch_1998. These are extreme cases, and they usually just hang about in one place, usually the romance section, where people just spend all the time talking dirty to each other.

There is of course much more that you can talk about. It is possible to visit a room and play a game of Trivial Pursuits, or your can go to the regional rooms, and talk to people who live in your area of the world. Most of the users on Yahoo are from America, where it is very popular, as they do not pay for their phone bills, unlike us.

In saying that, the phone bills do not have to be extortionate. Most Internet Service Providers (ISP) charge their phones at local rates, so at the weekend, it only costs you 1p per minute, and this wil be less if you put your ISP on your BT Friends & Family (which reminds me - I must do that sometime), so if you stay off of it during the week, it will work out to be a lot cheaper than a night in the pub (won't it Mark?).

I only have a few haunts that I frequent on Chat. I can usually be found anywhere in the Computer section - usually the Chat Help or Computer games. Failing that, it is either Triviall Pursuits or the UK regional site, where I have made a great many friends.

Many people also advertise their own website on the profile that is created when you sign on. Initally, when you first use it, you have the option of making a profile that other users can load in to find out more about who you are talking to. Most people are quite honest, like me - check up if you like, but some people who, lets say are male, make their profile up as if they were female, and even say they are female in the gender section. I cannot imagine who would do such a thing (would you, Mark?).

Anyway, all joking aside. It is a good way to make friends, and actually talk real time to someone In the USA without actually having to pay the full cost of the phone bill. Many of the "Internet weddings" that you hear about are instigated from this.

Not being one to hang about for long, and not liking Windoze 95 particularly, I decided to upgrade (can I call it that?) to Windoze 98. I must say that if you are connected to the Internet for long periods (like me!! - last bill was £203!!!!!!!!!!), then Win98 might be for you (assuming you own a PC!) The games that you get with the Plus Pack (yes, I bought that too!) are truly a great step up from the basic Patience and Minesweeper that you got with previous Windows versions. There is a "connect 5" style game, Loose Your Marbles, and it is truly brilliant. Also, you get those nice Themes as well, which means you can change all the sounds, icons, backgrounds etc, just by one click. Most are rubbish, but there are soon good ones out there. Best ones are on the Internet sites, which you should make an attempt to download.

You may be getting bored by all this Micro$oft buttering up by now, so time to change the tempo. Also I (well, my brother as well!) have taken the great step to install a truly brilliant Amiga Emulator on the PC. We are using the latest version of WinUAE, and it is brilliant. We now have a mini hard drive inside the great big PC one, where we store all our Amiga programs. We are already using OctaMED SoundStudio, Personal Paint V6.4, Voyager, Wordworth, and a few good PD games from my vast collection. Another upside is that I can view the PC hard drive from the Amiga side, and we are trying to get the Emulator to recognise the modem. We already have the mouse, monitor and CD Rom under our belt (the first time I have had an "Amiga" with a CD Rom - mag disks here we come!!!).

The good side to this is that I can now write the mag on the PC (I was using it mostly in the latter stages, although all the donkey work was done on the Amiga), and then load in the Amiga Emulator, and then Voyager to see what it would look like in an Amiga. This is not a strict truth, as we are running the Emulator in 16 bit colour, although I prefer 256 colour, as the emulator does get a bit slow at times trying to keep up.

Soon (probably by the time you read this) I will have our ZX Emulator, and probably Shapeshifter (MAC) running. So we can then run a Mac or ZX Spectrum on and Amiga on a PC!!!! Kewl or what?
