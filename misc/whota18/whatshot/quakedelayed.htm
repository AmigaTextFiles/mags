Quake Delayed & Quakeworld News


Quake Delayed.

Amiga Quake has been delayed slightly and it now set for release later in the month of February, as yet, and exact date has not been given.

It would seem that the game has been pretty much finished since the end of December, ClickBOOM just need a little more time to iron out some of the bugs and quirks that the game has. One particular thing that I hear they are paying attention to is making sure that all of the Quake add-ons work perfectly on the Amiga version.

Quakeworld.

Clickboom have also announed that ID have given the go-ahead to release Quakeworld for the Amiga. Quakeworld is a multi-player version of Quake designed for high latency TCP/IP connections. Quake supports multi-player support but games can lag badly unless you have a very high-speed connection. Quakeworld rectifies this and allows people woth a slower connections to enjoy the fun.

Quakeworld for the Amiga will be started as soon as Quake is finished and as with the original Quakeworld release it will be provided free of charge to Quake owners and won't be part of the actual Quake release. Looks out for more news on this soon.
