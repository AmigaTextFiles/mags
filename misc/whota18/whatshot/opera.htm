The following article contains information on a new web browser called Opera, from Opera Software.

By Helmar Rudolph, initiator of PM

Preliminary note: It is not too late to pledge your support! But read on first. Latest update here.

On October 10, Opera Software launched Project Magic, a unique initiative to gauge interest in Opera, our marvellous web browser, for operating systems other than MS Windows. We wanted to know from users of these OS...
  1. whether they would be interested in an alternative browser
  2. how much they'd be prepared to pay over and above the envisaged $35
  3. whether they'd be prepared to prefund the development of the products
Now this is not really common in software circles; neither the research method, nor asking for payment before the product has even been released - never mind designed. My initial thought was that there must be many users out there, tired of being caught in the browser war, tired of instable bloatware and 'kitchen sink' products that require more and more hardware just to surf (or is that 'paddle'?) the web. And boy was that assessment spot on!

In only seven weeks, we received in excess of 3000 emails from like-minded supporters, pledging orders for anything between 1 and 100 copies upfront - unseen! Some declared us crazy -- and we can't even fault them for that --, but the overwhelming majority was in support of Project Magic.

"I want to send a message to those browser giants that the free lunch is over, and that the time of Win dominated software development has seen its day. OS/2 and other alternative OS's are on the rise." - J. Jones
It wasn't only pledges we got; we also received hundreds of tips and tricks, contacts and suggestions -- information and intelligence companies would spend thousands of $$$ on. But to us it came all free of charge, only by showing our prospective customers that a) we can code (the product speaks for itself!) and b) that we really care.

Obviously, due to the nature of the project, we wanted to convince visitors that this initiative is worth supporting, and pointed them to our guestbook, to read what customers of ours had to say about the company and the product. Well, have a look yourself - it needs no commenting.

The initial idea was to get 3000 orders per platform, because that would have shown us that there is sufficient interest in these alternative Opera versions. 3000 only because the resulting $100k would have been enough to hire programmers to tackle the projects - and complete them -, but it was far more important to gauge market sentiment and price points than to reach the 3000.

"I think the problem with the freeware thing is that MSIE and Netscape (as free as it gets) are not what I think of as freeware; they're not gifts to the community, they're marketing tools for server software.

In some ways, distributing Opera as shareware (or whatever you're calling it) is good, it makes clear that how Netscape and Microsoft are abusing the concept of "freeware" is unacceptable." - D. Powers

I haven't counted the orders, only the emails, and although we hit 1200+ on both Mac and OS/2, the actual number of orders is about 75%-85% higher than the number of emails received. Needless to say that these two platforms enjoy priority, and we are currently busy negotiating with various parties to make these 'ideas' come true.

Linux? Well, personally, I am a bit disappointed by the response from the Linux users. Those that pledged were very (if not extremely) supportive, both with pledges and advice. But I expected more than the 500+ we got. Granted, our publicity was limited, but nevertheless, if there are millions of users out there, there surely must be 1000+ who'd be prepared to support a quality product such as Opera. Unfortunately not so; maybe there are too many flavours out there and a user base that is too used to free software. Although we have some very interesting pledges, this version may have to wait a while.

BeOS? Amazing; simply amazing. Not only did the BeOS initiative get off to a late start, but looking at the current status and user base of BeOS, I think the numbers of pledges are just superb. Not large in numbers, but compared to other operating systems, tried and tested, and with a user base in the millions, it was a sterling effort. In addition, the average pledge was about $20 more than we asked for. An efficient, streamlined browser for an efficient and streamlined OS? Sounds like an ideal match to me.

Thank you!

First of all I would like to extend my personal thanks to ALL of you who have supported Project Magic; be it with pledges, information, contacts, by putting the PM button onto your page and by (still) spreading the news amongst friends and colleagues.

I would also like to thank Richard Karpinski at InternetWeek for his article, as well as Trevor Smith, editor of OS/2 e- Zine! Special thanks go to Scot Hacker at BeHive and to Scott Peterson at Be, Inc.. Not to be forgotten... Guy Kawasaki, Apple Evangelist extraordinaire, and Lesley Pennington at Apple Europe..., thanks for your kind assistance and help!

"This sounds like a great product. Yes I would be willing to support it. Your approach seems refreshing and honest and your product reviews speak for themselves." - R. Welsh
Thank you also to all newsgroup moderators, who have posted PM announcements on the many groups out there.

Thank you to many more unnamed supporters and idea-providers. Thank you!

Last but not least, I would like to thank Jon von Tetzchner, Technical Director at Opera Software, for eventually giving in. It took me several weeks to convince him that we should be doing this, but I know how he feels now. Thanks, Jon. ;-) Thanks also to Sandra Thorbjørnsen for helping me wherever needed.

Last but not the very least, in my lunatic fit, I'd like to thank myself for accomplishing something other companies spend millions and millions on: creating awareness for› their product. Whereas they run expensive advertising campaigns, buy entire media, channels and middlemen to get the people use their products, I did it by simply 'hijacking' other platforms - by putting a Mercedes billboard into a BMW showroom. A simple yet brilliant idea at the right time and place - and it cost me nothing but some late nights in front of the PC and some serious surfing with Opera. ;-)

What you do if you have no money...

If you can't buy media at horrendous cost and with limited results, you need to think smart, and I think this has been accomplished full and well. With Project Magic I got thousands of people, who have never heard of Opera before, to come to our site and see what the 'Magic' is all about. And this was all I had to do, because once they've seen and tested the product, it was speaking for itself. No traditional advertising and PR needed. No fat budgets, no hyped-up creative nonsense.

Is this the way 'good' companies with 'excellent' products will do their marketing in the future? Could well be. But it for sure is a slap in the face of the big browser manufacturers. Not that I say this, but the user of their products (Have a look here to get ample proof). They might get ecstatic over market share percentages... 'which one is leading', 'which one can add more proprietary tags to make the Web even more (sub-)standard and hostile', 'which one can add more code-bloat that the user despises to the extreme'..., but they all fail to see that people are often using it because there's no alternative, not because the products are any good.

"I am so sick of the lethargic performance of Netscape, and I won't even consider the bloated and clunky IE, so of course I am all for a streamlined browser. In fact, I've been lamenting the lack of one since the demise of Mosaic. By all means, put me down as a MacOS supporter and pre-pledger." - Anon
The user feedback link above is just a tiny fraction of emails we got that express the user's dissatisfaction. "Instable, crashes often, slow, bloated, features I don't need, they never responded to emails I sent them" are but a few user sentiments that we received. We have hundreds more, many of them much harsher than these. Oh, and you should have seen the original name 'variations' users came up with... I won't mention them though. :->

But be it as it may, Project Magic was a sound success. And while I was spending endless nights to respond to several hundreds of emails, trying to allay fears, establishing new contacts and exploring new avenues, my dear friends eleven thousand kilometers further north spent endless nights improving the product - implementing user requests. Without them, PM would have never worked in the first place.

Opera 3.0 for Windows has just been officially release, with lots of new and really useful features and enhancements, and above all with a brandnew hotlist that will once again put all others to shame. And 'no', although we have added a new hotlist, Javascript, SSL, server push, Netscape plugins and a lot more, we have added a mere 400k to the 790k download size, making it a meagre 1.2MB+ download. Quality is definitely in the numbers (or lack therof).

The road ahead (pardon the pun)

Gosh, you have made it that far! ;-) The November 30, deadline was just an official cut-off date to count the numbers and to determine its success or failure, but not its officiall end. If you would like to support us, you can do this all the way until the final release! This page will stand as it is, but we will of course keep you posted here about the progress we are making.

We haven't yet decided whether to actually ask for upfront payment, but we will be contacting you if this becomes reality.

All in all, you have shown us that there is a dire need for a small and efficient browser, and we hear you loud and clear. We thank you for your support and appreciate your further input.

Opera's Features

At a time when almost every software upgrade requires an often significant investment in new hardware, you will just love to have come across Opera, our refreshingly different Internet Client.

Opera has a number of features that undoubtedly make it the Number One for the serious online computer user.

Features & Benefits

  • Opera sticks to the HTML standard

    It is becoming a nightmare for website developers to make their pages 'best viewed' with one of the big browsers. Using Opera, you know exactly that your pages are best viewed with any browser. You will soon use Opera as your standard reference browser to make sure that your pages are open to all, and not just to those with a specific browser.

  • Sophisticated and speedy web browsing with Usenet newsgroups and simple Email.

    Although pretty simple, the mail and news features will be extended and enhanced in the near future. We may even spin them off into separate modules. This will then mean one small, fast, lean and mean program for all your needs. And if you are an advanced user with your own email and newsreader, Opera links to all of them!

  • Opera is extremely small and very fast.

    It also runs on older PCs; a 386SX with 8MB RAM is already sufficient. Do you know what that really means? The best possible protection of your computer investment. Not really a big issue when it's just a home PC, but if you are in a corporation with hundreds and thousands of PCs, using Opera translates into saving millions on upgrade costs. And with the trend going towards a NetPC, Opera might be your lifesaver.

  • Opera is your product.

    Dozens of customer suggestions have found their way into Opera. We are very keen to get your input on Opera, because we are developing it for you, and no-one else. We have a questionnaire online, so if you'd like to tell us a few things, fire right away!

  • Opera is ideal for disabled users.

    Using Opera as a disabled user means being able to access the Net quickly and efficiently. Only Opera makes it possible to navigate entirely with the keyboard, bringing joy and happiness to many thousands of visually or physically handicapped friends all over the world.

  • Opera is flexible.

    Whether you are a looking for an Internet browser or an Intranet client, an info kiosk, CD-ROM front-end or offline browser, Opera can be tailor-made to suit your company's needs. You can develop your content in HTML, and publish it using Opera. Quick, cost-effective and reliable. And with all the benefits a full screen has compared to a fixed resolution!

  • Opera is unique.

    Whether it's the customisation or the online graphics toggling, the multiple windows with individual settings, or the superb if different address list, Opera offers features you won't find anywhere else.

  • Opera is powerful.

    With version 3.0 JavaScript and server push, SSL and Netscape are all included in a small download package. This means access to more websites, online banking and secure transfers without additional modules, and more diversity and versatility while browsing. Obviously, all 'new features' can be turned off any time.

  • Opera is simple.

    Installation is a breeze and takes less than 30 seconds. It doesn't eat up your hard disk space either. Requirements are about 1.7MB.

But there is more:

  • Opera is written from scratch and is not based on the code of Mosaic or any other browser. Opera can run independently under Windows 3.1 and does not require Win32s.
  • You can choose font type, color and other presentation parameters for headers (H1 - H6), normal and preformatted text, forms and newsgroups.
  • Opera has advanced link presentation: Choose color, 3D button, bold, underline, strikethrough, etc.
  • Opera lets you link to your favourite email client if you so wish.
  • Opera lets you save global and local home pages.
  • Opera lets you retrieve all elements of a hotlist (bookmarks) with one click.
  • Opera lets you retrieve documents in the background, without resizing/moving your current document window.
  • and much more...

Window Management

With Opera you can view multiple documents at the same time. The documents are shown within a familiar user interface where you can easily tile and cascade the documents. Opera even has a built-in auto-tile function, which, if you prefer, can also switch off. Opera has a zooming function and supports full keyboard navigation, which makes it ideal for visually impaired net surfers. In fact, it's the only serious web browser that can offer this!

Opera fetches multiple documents and images at the same time. The documents are loaded faster and you need not wait for a single document. You can just browse in another window.

Multiple downloads are also possible at the same time - all in one special download window.

Start with multiple windows
Opera can start up with a number of windows. Opera can also start with the same setup as when you last exited. Each window can be with or without graphics and have its own home page. Other settings for the window are stored as well.

These window settings can also be restored while the program is running, adding extra power and flexibility to your web surfing.

Opera and the Operating System

Opera loves small computers
Opera runs quite happily on a 386SX with 8MB of memory! It will also run on machines with less memory (4 MB), but will quickly run out of space.

No winsock required
Opera runs quite happily without network access. Opera does not require a winsock.dll. Use Opera to access your files at home or on a CD

Windows 95/98/NT 4.0
What a question! Of course, Opera is available as a native 32bit version.

Windows 3.X
Opera also available as a native 16 bit application running under Windows 3.1 and 3.11. No win32s is required.

OS/2
Opera runs quite well on OS/2 and it runs fast.

Very flexible user interface.

Choose whether to show menus, buttons, scrollbars, progress information etc. If you don't need it, why show it?

  • Decide presentation parameters: Decide font type, color and other presentation parameters for headers (H1 - H6), normal and preformatted text, form buttons and form fields, newsgroup headers, group header and message text.
  • Advanced link presentation: Choose color, 3D button, underline, strikethrough, etc.

Support for multiple graphics formats

  • Support for JPEG, GIF, XBM and BMP bitmap formats.
  • Animated GIF supported.

Multimedia

  • Inline support for AVI video (requires Video for Windows 1.1).
  • Inline support for MPEG video (requires MPEG software or hardware).
  • Background sounds and MIDI music supported. All multimedia can be turned off!

Modern and Feature- rich

  • Javascript (only when needed)
  • SSL 2.0 and 3.0, plus TLS 1.0 - 128bit encryption (only when needed)
  • Full HTML 2.0 support.
  • Most of HTML 3.2 implemented.
  • Client Side ISMAP - use local clickable images with multiple hot spots.
  • Client pull automatic loading of documents is supported.
  • Server Push.
  • Full table and forms support.
  • FONT tag with color and size attribute supported.
  • FONT FACE support.
    This is Times New Roman.
    And this is MS Sans Serif
  • Sophisticated hotlist.
  • Secure News server access
  • Popup URLs - Selectable popup link information. Now you know where that link leads before you click it.
  • Context sensitive menus
  • Separate menus for documents, images and links.
  • Automatic window placement (This option lets Opera auto-arrange windows so that the full Opera window is always filled with windows).

Integrated Web, News and simple mail

Opera integrates web and news in a seamless user interface. This also includes a simple mail window for sending messages to persons or to news groups. The news system allows you to use multiple windows for groups and messages. Each news window has its own button bar for quick navigation and management.

Sound on actions

With Opera you can use a sound to indicate that you have clicked a link and another sound to indicate that a dopcument has finished loading. A sound for failed loading, startup and exit can be selected as well.

System administrators' dream

With Opera you can choose to disable some of the user preferences and override user choices. Used with care this system is useful for hiding items froms the users; including proxy servers and network choices.

Global hot list
In addition to the user hotlist, Opera has a system hot list. This hot list can be managed by the system administrators and include items that are useful for all users. This is another unique feature of Opera.

Flexible startup
You can start Opera with different setup files as a parameter in addition to up to 10 addresses (URLs). This makes it easier for system administrators to check out different setups without destroying their own.
