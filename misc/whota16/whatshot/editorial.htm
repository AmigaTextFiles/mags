This issue's editorial for the What's Hot On The Amiga Magazine, concentrates on a lot of things, including the release of Index Information's new Amiga Compatibles, the Access and the Boxer, with exclusive articles on both them appearing in this issue with images. And with the release of the Boxer and the Access, I can't wait to see Index Information's InsideOut which nothing has been heard about this for some time and is there answer to a next generation Amiga. But, yet again, Foundation gets a mention in this issue in the News In Brief as does OnEscapee, of which nothing has been heard about for a while now.

What's Hot On The Amiga Magazine can still be found on the web, at:

http://www.sonic.net/~al anwall/whota/index.html.

This issue contains a survey, which I hope you'll fill in and send back to me as I was asked by Geosync Media before whota15, to provide information of the specifications of the Amigas that you're using currently. So far there have been some responses, but I'm including the survey in this issue in the hope that more people will respond to it, and as always, the survey is also available at the web site.

This issue's biggest article must be ClickBOOM Statement On Quake Situation, with the Sadeness Software signs Forgotten Forever and Dafel:Bloodline!articles and the Alive MediaSoft articles, Alive MediaSoft Sign Up Gilbert Goodmate and Alive MediaSoft To Publish Goblin Tanks being of interest as well.

Forgotten Forever, the Amiga's answer to Command & Conquer on the PC is also mentioned in the Forgotten Forever - New Screenshots article and the New Screenshots of Hard Target article are definitely worth a look as well.

Now this month's, Site Of The Month is CU-Amiga, which at the moment has got a couple of surveys on it, for you to fill in and is always worth a visit.
