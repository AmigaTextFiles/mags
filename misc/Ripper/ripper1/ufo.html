UFO Enemy Unknown

INTRODUCTION

On any clear night, look at the eerie starry sky and say, "who am I? Why am I here? Is there anybody out there?" Humans have always felt awe, fear and wonder for the infinite blackness os space, but as science has replaced prehistoric religion, we have replaced the Ancient Ones with the Alien Ones. And this superb tactical game of sci-fi combat and strategy from Microprose sends you head-to-head against them.

You play the commander of XCOM, an elite secret multi-national sub-group of the United Nations. Various countries fund your work on the rapidly increasing hostile alien activity. You must shoot down alien spacecraft, kill or capture the surviving occupants and investigate any equipment recovered. UFO is very successful on two distinct levels. The first involves the building and managing bases around the world, developing facilities and maintaining an effective military presence. What and where you build is upto you, but failure to deal with alien incursions will mean a reduction in funding from the country being bothered. Failure to offer adequate protection may ultimately mean they sign a pact with the aliens, further adding to your woes. Your base can comprise of several different facility types including hangars, living quarters, stores, radar stations, laboratories, workshops and alien containment units. They all cost money to run and it may be necessary to supplement a bases running costs by selling alien artifacts, equipment and tools.

Employing scientists means you can research alien technology while engineers can manufacture it. You can also perform autopsies on dead aliens, interrogate live ones and bit by bit your understanding of their culture will increase. Pretty soon you'll be throwing away your M16 rifles and Auto- cannons in favour of power-armour, plasma guns and blaster launches. The latter is a devastating smart-missile launcher and the pinnacle of alien achievement.

The other level of the game is more tactical. Once a spaceship has been sighted and shot down you must despatch troops to investigate and kill the survivors. Crash sites are the missions encountered most, but the same multi-level 3D isometric perspective is used when your base is attacked, you engage a "terror site" or you assault a secret enemy installation. Each soldier has a rank based upon his experience, number of kills and previous mission ratings. His or her ability to perform is affected by a number of factors from morale to health, but mainly by the number of Time Units available. These determine how many actions can be undertaken in a round of combat. Actions such as walking, kneeling, aiming, shooting all take Time Units and this makes the game very tactical.

It's impossible to list all the features and options covered in this comprehensive game. Graphically it's superb, the music and sound effects are outstanding and there's a long sustained challenge with several levels of difficulty. I can't recommend this title enough. If you like strategy, 3D combat and tactical gameplay, you won't be disappointed. This must rate as one of the best games in its genre.

Verdict: "Unreservedly recommended!"
