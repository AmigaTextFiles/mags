Chip manufacturing and Overclocking (28-Aug-97 )

    Philipp Boerker points out that all Motorola chips are manufactured using one single process and are only assigned a MHz rating after passing the respective tests. The changing quality during manufacturing may be an explanation why some 060s get extremely hot while others don't. If there where no flaws during manufacturing, the chip may be overclocked by up to 100%, but there is no way to find out the quality of the manufacturing of a 060 and therefore it is not suggested to overclock the chips.

Haynie's Acutiator (26-Aug-97 )

    Dave Haynie says in 1991 he was developing a new overall system architecture for the Amiga, code-named "Acutiator". It was a highly modular architecture, with each piece indepently replacable, a CPU-independent motherboard and a new inter-module bus called AMI bus (Amiga Modular Interconnect). The AMI bus was supposed to be small (few pins), fast (100 MB/s) and auto-configuring. In 1992 PCI was announced, which was basically better in every area than the AMI bus, but in addition it was also backed by half the industry. By the fall of '93, Commodore was too broke to spend much money on AAA or Hombre. The PIOS One is basically what happens when you try to build an Acutiator type system entirely from off-the-shelf parts.
     · Posted by Dave Haynie on Wed, 20 Aug 1997 03:10:28 GMT in comp.sys.amiga.misc [L].

MUI using co-operative multitasking? (04-Aug-97 )

    According to Collin Ward, MUI uses co-operative multitasking. Whenever it processes input.device related calls, there can't be a switch to another process, the system locks up until input.device is released (by MUI). Collin thinks this is a fundamental design problem that needs to be addressed; as a mouse pointer locking up in IBrowse when a jpeg is loaded is a PC/Windows 3.1 type behaviour.

    Phase5 have laboratory equipment worth 0,5 million DEM (± 300,000 USD), with 200 channel logic analyser, 16 channel 2 GHz logicscope, more logic analysers and scopes, EMV-measurement devices, at least 10 test computers, about 40 standard hardware products for compatibility tests, lots of laboratory material (that alone worth the purchase price of a car).
