The Heckler (by Ralf Driesner)

Flying Horses

Noise in the house .... slowly I wake up. It is a Saturday morning and I had a hard Friday night. It is Beaujolais Primeur time on planet Earth. This wonderful occasion has thrown its spell over me, too.

First I open the left eye, then the right one. I try to catch a clear thought. What happened last night?! Into the hard and cruel reality of a Saturday morning slowly tiptoe memories of many senseless words which this dainty red wine elicited from me.

Then events go head over heels .... the phone rings, the alarm-clock rattles, the doorbell chimes .... and in my head it rings, rattles, chimes as well .... everything is ringing, the whole world!!


I stay in bed in spite of this torture. A thought flashes through my mind: Who the hell is taking the risk of bothering me!?! And so many at the same moment ... 
 
I try to stretch my arms. My face, distorted by a juicy yawning, must look excruciating. But no worries. I don't give a hoot about the troublemakers and compose my emotions for my breakfast. I decide to elect this idea to be the best of the young day. Even though I know that my gustatory neural system will play havoc with me. 
 
I open the can with the breakfast meat .... mmmhhh, looks like the fodder of my cat which deceased a few years ago. That roll has seen better days as well. And the coffee ... so what, it can prepare itself. 
 

As my sensorial cells pine for "the good ole days", when I did not have to spend a single thought on shopping (food didn't taste any better in those days, but I could put the blame on somebody else), I catch an idea shuffling past: I sure hope OS 3.9 doesn't work like my breakfast tastes.

Little differences undoubtedly exist: The screenshots on the Internet don't look like cat's fodder. And OS 3.9 will come in a cardboard box. But I can't get rid of the thought that they want to sell us something out of a can. My "girlfriend", her parts slowly breaking down, is to get a new heart. Or, in better words, a new cardiac pacemaker for her old heart (Kick 3.1-ROMs). And the price for this is about 80 German pebbles?


I don't want to rehash the old discussion "Amiga software always was a bit more expensive because of its low sales numbers", when I exceptionally disclaim the price to be adequate. Anyhow, the suspicion lingers that behind the claim "Whether there will be an OS 4.0 for the Classic will only be decided by the sales numbers of OS 3.9" may be the idea, finally, to make bangers out of that old horse. I believe a lower price would have been appropriate. Just to get better sales numbers .... 
 

Now back to breakfast meat, my actual problem. The thought about horse bangers has definitively spoiled my appetite. Especially because the smell of this "somewhat" in the can really does not motivate consumption.

Faintly I moan to myself .... hoping OS 3.9 will become better.

Live long and prosperous (preferably without sausage out of the can)
