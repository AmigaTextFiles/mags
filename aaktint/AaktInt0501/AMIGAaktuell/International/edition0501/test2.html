Test reports

2. Addition to the BoingBag 3.9.1 review (by Christian Aichinger)

Well, what shall I say? One day after I mailed the BoingBag-review to Carsten, there was a reworked BB1 available on the server at Amiga. If there hasn't been a message at amiga-news.de, perhaps no-one had noticed it, because neither the versionnumber was different from the first BB1, nor there were large differences in the archive size. This shows once again the "funny" information policy of Amiga. On the other hand, one has to praise the fast reaction to bug-reports and the fast release of BB1 (or not praise: read the reports of readers who wrote me).

Unfortunately, I made a mistake, too. The shown command line to copy utilities.catalog on the os3.9-cdrom to fix the IconEdit-bug was wrong. It must be: 

"copy amigaos3.9:OS-Version3.9/Locale/Catalogs/deutsch/Sys/utilities.catalog
Locale:Catalogs/deutsch/sys/"

Meanwhile, because of the reworked BB1 this bug should be gone.

To the new BB1:

The mentioned problems with the german utilities.catalog and IconEdit have been fixed and also some updated Commodities have been accommodated in the new BB1.

No-one has reported anything about the mentioned problems about integrating AMPlifier into Voyager and I had not enough time to wrestle with that problem. As soon as I find any time for that and maybe find a solution (or an attentive reader sends me all necessary information), I will keep you informed.

The second mentioned problem with HDToolBox has been discovered by me now: As my SCSI-hostadapter (Blizzard-1230IV-SCSI-Kit) broke down last month and I had to remove it, there was a wrong (better: non-existing) device-driver (1230scsi.device) entered in the tooltype-settings of HDToolBox.

After deleting this tooltype, HDToolBox worked properly. I cannot say if it was a bug in HDToolBox or a wrong configuration done by me. A little information from HDToolBox, that the entered device-driver is not available, would have been nice. There is no reason for making things more complex than they must be.

Some readers have reported their experiences with BoingBag1 for OS3.9 and I should pass them on to you:

pinned, no graphics-output)
>the computer a couple of times.

For the slow ASyncWB I have to say it needs a fast harddisc-controller. With a zorroII-IDE-controller or the internal IDE-adapter for the A1200, the cpu will be busy with ASyncWB a lot. I discovered that when I had to do without my SCSI-adapter and only had a 2.5"-IDE-harddisc on hand. On the other hand with a working SCSI-adapter ASyncWB's speed is absolutely satisfactory. That the whole system freezes because of ASyncWB is not correct anyway. Perhaps increasing buffers for the different partitions using the AddBuffers-command or with HDToolBox may help. Numbers around 1000 should lead to a noticeable performance increase.
