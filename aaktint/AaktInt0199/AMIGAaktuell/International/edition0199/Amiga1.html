1. Mystery of 'MMC' manufacturer solved?

The mystery of the manufacturer of the so-called 'MMC' media processor, that is expected to be used in the next-generation-Amiga is most likely to be solved: With reference to Amiga International, Inc. a user reported in a public Amiga forum, that the firm was bought by the canadian graphic hardware manufacturer ATI, recently. If this is true the only conclusion is that the manufacturer of the 'MMC' is Chromatic Research (http://www.chromatic.com), ATI bought in November 1998. Even months ago Chroatic Research was named as manufacturer of the 'MMC', but only in rumours. More detailed informations to the mediaprocessor MPact are available under http://www.mpact.com.

It is not certain, if Amiga, Inc. is going to use the technology of Chromatic Research after ATI bought it. But at least they do not commit on this, but develop the new AmigaOS hardware indipendent.

Here are three press releases regarding the buying of Chromatic Research by ATI:

» ATI Technologies Inc. Agrees to Acquire Chromatic Research, Inc. Core 
technology for System-on-a-Chip designs used in sub $500 Computers, 
Set-Top Boxes and Consumer Devices 
 

Toronto, Canada, October 21, 1998 - ATI Technologies Inc. (TSE:ATY) the world leader in 3D graphics/video acceleration and multimedia solutions, today announced it has entered into a definitive agreement to acquire Chromatic Research, Inc. for a total investment of US$67 million, comprised of 100% of the outstanding shares of Chromatic and assumption of Chromatic's net liabilities.

The purchase is consistent with ATI's long history of integrating core technology blocks and responding to key market inflection points. Chromatic is currently developing next-generation System-on-a-Chip (SOC) technology, a highly integrated product that encompasses graphics, system logic and processor blocks into a single chip. Chromatic's next-generation technology is an ideal candidate for numerous multimedia applications incorporated in sub-$500 computers, set-top boxes as well as other consumer electronic devices.

"This move is intended to extend our lead in the graphics and multimedia industry", said KY Ho, ATI's president and CEO. "Chromatic has a history of developing excellent technology. ATI has the infrastructure and economies of scale to enable success in this new product category."

"Chromatic's development to date adds significantly to our technology portfolio and allows us to extend our competencies into fast growing markets," said Adrian Hartog, ATI's vice-president, engineering. "This higher level of integration allows us to address fast growing segments in the sub $500 computer, set-top box and consumer electronics industries."

ATI will be acquiring all in-process research and development related to future products of Chromatic, equal to more than 300 man-years of both hardware and software engineering, all rights to their intellectual property including a significant patent portfolio relating to over 100 inventions and fixed assets. Chromatic currently employs approximately 140 engineers. The purchase of Chromatic will be financed through cash. The transaction is expected to close, subject to Chromatic's shareholder and the necessary regulatory approvals, on or about November 13, 1998.

About Chromatic Founded in 1993, Chromatic Research develops media processor technology for use in low cost PCs, set-top boxes, and consumer electronics products. The company is privately held with headquarters in Sunnyvale, California.
