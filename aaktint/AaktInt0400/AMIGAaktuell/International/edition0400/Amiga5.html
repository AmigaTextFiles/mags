AMIGA

5. News in Brief

  • On the "Amiga 2k" show it was told, that the rights of the 68k-Amiga clone BoXeR, which was announced for years, were sold to the American company Anti-Gravity (http://www.antigravity.com). The device will be produced under control of Mick Tinker in the US. In the media, a price of 1895 US-Dollars (approx. 2037.63 Euros) [Remark of the editor: but who is going to buy the device for that price?].

  • And then there is another info: The computer case designs shown on the show photos (and criticized by many people) are supposed not to be the Disney designs, but the work of Amiga users.

  • AMIGA reduced prices: The A1200 is very often for approx. 200 DEM (approx. 102.04 Euros) available, the A1200 HD for approx. 250 DEM (approx. 127.55 Euros).

  • Those of you, who are interested to work in the developer department of AMIGA, can apply by sending an eMail to developer@amiga.com.

  • Former people engaged on Amiga, who left the system in the meantime, but would like to return should send an eMail to friends@amiga.com.
