4. Games news from all over the world

  • Heretic II demo released

At last, this month it was done: The most awaited Amiga game has been released in a playable demo version. The demo of Heretic II of Hyperion Software has approx. 40 MB and requires a powerful Amiga. These are the system requirements:

  • PowerPC processor
  • WarpOS4
  • 64 MB RAM
  • 70 MB of free hard disk space
  • AGA or graphics board
  • AHI 4
Optional is a Warp3D compatible graphics board for 3D acceleration. The demo version can be downloaded from the homepage of Hyperion Software.

  • Hyperion Software recapitulates

Soon, Hyperion Software will have their first birthday and take this chance to get a result. In this time, Hyperion did impressive work; so some standards of the PC world like 3D games or video codings, which allow it to port PC games much easier, because the desired developer interfaces already exist on the Amiga, were ported to the Amiga. In addition to that a FAQ was published on the homepage of Hyperion Software. We can take a good look on the products of Hyperion Software, because due to their work Hyperion is the great white hope in matters of Amiga games.

  • Nightlong nearly completed

clickBOOM announced, that the Amiga port of Nightlong is nearly ready. In the first days of April there were unfortunately only 150 pre-orders for the PowerPC version of Nightlong, which is not many, but clickBOOM will continue the PPC development and hopes for new pre-orders. There is also a new trailer for Nightlong on the portal of clickBOOM. The completed version of Nightlong is supposed to be released within the next weeks. You can read a test here, then.

  • Apano Sin now from V.A.K. Entertainment and CS&E

Apano Sin, which was originally released by Alive Mediasoft will be released from V.A.K. Entertainment in Germany and from CS&E in Great Britain within the next weeks. Apano Sin is a classic Shoot 'em up with 6 different worlds, which have to be completed by the player.
