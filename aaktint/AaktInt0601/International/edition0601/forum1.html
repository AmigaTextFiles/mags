1. More opinions about BoingBag3.9.1-Review and several Solutions for Problems (by Christian Aichinger)

Well, it seems, my review angered several AmigaOS betatesters, even though I never intended to. The addendum for BB1, which appeared in the last edition, perhaps gave the impression to some readers that I do not rate the OS3.9 update highly. But this is not true. I am very content with OS3.9 and the BB3.9.1 has filled my expectations nearly completely.

Concerning the contents of letters to the editor, I want to state that I publish letters always (if they follow the rules of common taste and if I have the permission to do so) and I do not censor them (at the most I may comment on them). That the letters published in the last addendum threw discredit üupon the BB3.9.1, may have been caused by my request in the review of BB3.9.1 to report problems with the update and, if possible, point out solutions. That only three readers came forward is more an argument for the quality of the BoingBag than against it.

Michael Christoph <michael@meicky-soft.de> sent me a mail answering some of my points of criticism. My commentaries are enclosed in square brackets.

Gernod Schomberg in an addendum to the BB3.9.1 review had mentioned the problems with the new WBRun command and AsyncWB. As I wrote, this led to a common troubleshooting. I do not want to keep back the two mails of Gernod and Michael. Here is the mail from Michael Christoph:

> Hi Christian,
>
> now I have investigated both the error causes with Gernod. Here are the
> results:
>
> WBRun cannot launch programs without the workbench.library, which is
> only activated when the Workbench runs. Therefore it is in principle
> not possible (and this never was intended) to start programs from the
> User-Startup. Solution: Use "system-hacking" WBRun (e.g. from Fish Disk
> 757) or normal RUN.
>
> AsyncWB works faultlessly - here the problem is the Workbench as well.
> There is no solution until now - but Olaf Barthel will look into this
> matter more deeply. By the way, the problem is the duplication of files
> in the same partition - from one partition to the other multi-copy is
> possible without problems.
> Solution: Duplicate files/drawers one by one (even if I cannot
> understand, why copies should be created in the same partition).

And here the mail of Gernod:

> Hello Christian, (Your message from 12-May-01)
>
> after several messages to and fro we have reached a satisfying result.
> Michael Christoph recognized what had caused the deficiencies I had
> encountered, and found some more, which I was not able to see.
> For instance, he had sent me a not yet published version of "WBRun",
> which regularly aborted with "Datei nicht ausführbar" ["File not
> executable"] on my computer. After several questions and answers he
> sent me the file, now packed together with "SnoopDOS", which indeed
> I already had in a later version, and this "WBRun" responded correctly.
>
> I already had prepared an answer, in which I had enclosed both WBRuns
> with different names, for a test I unpacked them and now both of them
> worked alright! Now I checked the originals and found out, that they
> only differed by the protection bit "e", which was set in one version,
> but not in the other! The packers had manipulated the protection bit.
>
> Michael was quite baffled that this is a problem in OS3.9, for
> until now the ability to execute a program was determined only by
> the start-bytes of the program itself. And the reason, why the new
> "WBRun" does not run out of the "User-Startup", apparently is, that
> the WB is not opened yet and with this the appropriate library not
> active.
>
> He also handed over the matter with the multi-copy of files inside
> a directory to other programmers.
>
> So again the gentlemen have something to think over,
>
>
> With best regards,
>
> Gernod

Concerning the reworked version of WBRun: Users with problems with this new command should report to the support addresses named above (and additionally collected at the end of this text) and just ask for a reworked version. As you can conclude from the commentaries, the members of the OS3.9 team are ready to help with problems. To report a problem still is the task of the user. The members of the OS3.9 support still are not "clairvoyants" ;).

Finishing, I'd like to express that it was not a simple task to collect all letters, which I had received, into a common (con-)text. But I hope I could condense the answers in a satisfying way and must not write another addendum ;).
