But now, last month's solutions:

Standard question: At the greatest (not only Computer-) show in the world 
 happening before long the first handheld device based 
 upon AmigaDE will be presented. What is the name of 
 this show? 
  
Solution: Of course we asked for the CeBIT, the biggest show in 
 the world. 
  
Master question: Which company will release this handheld device to 
 the market? 
  
Solution: Correct answer: »Thendic Electronic Components«. 
  
Question of opinion: Will you visit the show we are talking about? 
  
Answer Number of Nominations 
1 (Yes) 5 
2 (No) 30 
  

Several participants added comments to their answers to the question of opinion:

Karl-Heinz Bader answers the question, whether he will visit the CeBIT in a, well, let me call it quite interesting, way: »Probably not. But maybe after all yes? :))«

Krister S. Skrtic answers "No" and states: »If only we had a similar show here in Gothenburg, Sweden! I certainly should visit it!«

Stephan Karnasch likewise will not come, because he is in the middle of preparations for his A-Level exam.

Gernod Schomberg as well will not go to the CeBit: »To keep my professional career on track I felt obliged to visit the Hannover show every year. I did it once many years ago, had to visit business partners, saw just two of them in one day, and afterwards I was so worn out by all the walking around, that I was happy to head home by Intercity Express train in the late night! In the years after that I made a habit of telling my boss about a colleague much better qualified than I, who was enthusiastic to go to the show.

Besides, I always received the show info from our business partners and suppliers beautifully presented, much better than I could have done it.

And why should I today, a pensioner, endure all this grief when all the media carry detailed reports about it?«

Net result:

I was quite surprised by the low interest among participants in the CeBIT. Not just because of the possible presentation of the first AmigaDE products this year, but because I generally assumed that the biggest fair of the world had a high value among Amiga users, too. But this seems to be a mis-assessment, perhaps because the CeBIT more and more has the image of a »Professional Fair« in its latest years.
