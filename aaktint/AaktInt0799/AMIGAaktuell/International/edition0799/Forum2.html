2. 'Next Level' reports about the Amiga (by Wolfgang W. Wolber)

»The multiformat game console mag "Next Level" (XL) also reports about the future plans of Amiga in their column "32-Bit-Level -Behind The Scenes-" in their June/July 99 edition. 
 

Under the headline "nicht totzukriegen" ("not to get dead") they report about the announcement of AMIGA to present a new product family including a handheld Internet computer and an A500 successor.

They also report, that the Amiga 1000 was the first 32-bit computer in 1985.

In addition there is a small picture of the Boing ball (but named as "boink" ;-)) and the Amiga logo (cut out from an AI poster. They also mentioned the Internet addresses of AMIGA.

In the Past the Amiga was mentioned a few times. In the readers corner some editors "outed" themselves as (former) Amiga users and in a test of the Team17 games X2 the pole position of the Amiga in sound and graphics in the beginnings of the 90s was mentioned in a review of the predecessor Project-X.

In the emulators corner of the XL (which was under charge of J. Eggebrecht from Factor 5) our lady appeared sometimes.

Wolfgang W. Wolber <wolfgang.wolber@okay.net>«
