1. Response to a Comment concerning »Virtual Processor« (by Dirk Wenk)

Dirk Wenk in the October edition commented on his answer to the question of opinion in the last but one competition as follows:

»[...] Ideally the AmigaOS/OE runs upon a processor understanding the VP code directly. You may imagine a CPU like the one of Transmeta but instead of emulating a x86 architecture it emulates a VP CPU! [...]«

The chief editor of Amiga aktuell annotated:

»Would this really make sense? One of the special advantages of the new AmigaOS is the fact that it includes a virtual processor and thus may be used on many types of processors without a major loss of speed. In my view, especially because of this technology a processor like the Crusoe, which follows a similar route - but hardware based - is not necessary in an AmigaNG computer.«

Now Dirk again answers:

»This is true to some degree ... The translating matrix for the VP is in the L2 cache, whereas for the processor it could be situated directly in the internal memory ... Ok, the VP really is fast, no doubt, but why not?


A system containing two CPUs might be conceivable; as an example: The first one is a normal x86 CPU and the second one a Crusoe with VP emulation. 
 

Of course the VP is hosted before the processors ... for the x86 it transforms the statements to enable the x86 to understand them ... for the Crusoe it directly forwards them.

But in the meantime I am in doubt concerning the Crusoe ... in the latest c't [German computer periodical] a test is printed ... I believe, the hosted VP is faster than a Crusoe VP emulation.

Well, let's wait and see!«

Dirk Wenk <dw@teleservice.com>«
