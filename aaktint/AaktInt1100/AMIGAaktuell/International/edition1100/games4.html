4. Games News from all over the World

  • Nightlong is here!

A few days ago it came true: The release of probably the first Amiga game on 3 CD ROMs. "Nightlong" is a high-end adventure game and will be scrutinized closely in the next edition of AMIGA aktuell. Already the first update to cure a few bugs has been published.

  • Demo version of ACSYS is published

This month after all the waiting a playable demo version of the Turrican clone ACSYS was released. And you betcha, the waiting was worth it! Preview in this edition.

  • Foundation Gold - Free of Charge?

This is possible with this edition of AMIGA aktuell. Just have a look into the Competition section! You could win "Foundation Gold".

  • Last Word from CS&E

It became known two months ago that CS&E would withdraw from the Amiga market and now David Connolly has made a final statement. He states that it simply does not pay any more to offer commercial products for the Amiga. He dissociates himself as well from the New Amiga. From now on CS&E only will produce for the PC. 
But it now appears that Amiga games will after all be released in Germany. For this purpose a contract was signed with Dirk Harlaar, who will take over the distribution. 
 
  • Fubar

After a long wait, some progress on "Fubar". The developers team, which now is named "Bartman Software", have provided a beta version on their homepage for download. Before downloading you have to register.

  • Europa Universalis

Canadian company TribSoft Inc. secured from Paradox Entertainment the licence to port their game to Linux, Mac and Amiga. TribSoft Inc. are experts in the field of Linux/Mac ports. The Amiga version will be ported by Hyperion Software.

  • Earth 2140 Demo Version

A playable demo version of the long expected Command & Conquer clone "Earth 2140" finally was released this month. It fills a lazy 20 Megabytes and needs at least a 68040/25Mhz, a graphics board and 24 MBytes RAM. In this edition is a preview of this demo.

  • Majesty for the AmigaNG

Titan Computer acquired a licence to port the strategic game "Majesty" to the AmigaNG. The technical part of the port will be done by Hyperion Software. "Majesty" is to be released in 2001.

  • Hellcoders announce new Game

The Dutch developers team Hellcoders announced a new shoot'em'up titled "HellFight". The game is said to be a classic shoot'em'up with some additional thrilling tasks. Currently they are working on porting the graphics and intermediate sequences.

  • Exodus

During this month a playable demo version of the Command & Conquer clone "Exodus - The Last War" was published. The demo version spans 14 Megabytes compressed, and I would have offered you a preview, but that was not possible because the server was permanently overloaded. The distribution of this real-time strategic game will be in the hands of Blittersoft.

  • The World Foundry

The World Foundry appears to have withdrawn from the Amiga market and may be closing down communication generally. Amiga Future reported that the development of the games "Maim & Mangle" and "Explorer 2260" is continuing independently under programmer Chris Page. The responsibility of The World Foundry has ended. Furthermore the website http://www.worldfoundry.com/ has disappeared. All the signs point to The World Foundry liquidating, if they have not done so already.
