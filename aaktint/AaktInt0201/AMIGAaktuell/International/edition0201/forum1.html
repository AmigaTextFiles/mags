1. Installation Tips for WinUAE - Test of Edition 01/2001 (by Thomas Graf)

I have read the article Denis Nikolics wrote about WinUAE, too. I also am a supporter. It is not a genuine Amiga, but on a fast Laptop it is real fun. Right now I am travelling in Amerika/Hawaii and cannot be parted from my emulated Amiga. :)

I have read that Denis had problems with AmigaOS 3.9. I have overcome all problems with the installer. Here a few important hints:

  • 100% sound emulation must be activated, because the installer wants to play sound with sound datatype.

  • At first copy the CD via WinUAE to a .hdf partition. Make the appropriate assign and after that you must make all files readable, executable and writeable with the command "Protect AmigaOS39:#? rwed ADD ALL"

  • Then start the installer and let it run. Cancel the last step, before it wants to make a new start (important, because it produces a steady reset otherwise).

  • Open the Startup-Sequence with an editor and change the line "c:setpatch QUIET" to "c:setpatch SKIPROMUPDATES QUIET". By doing this you prevent the installation of the scsi.device and the Fast File System. These are incompatible with WinUAE. So far I can't find out why.

That's it. Everything else works just perfectly. If you have a fast enough PC, there's no probs playing MP3s. :))

If you have questions or advice, send them to me. I'm keen to learn more.

By the way, the Cloanto-FAQ's are not bad, but in parts incomplete. Another good source is this Newsgroup: http://amiga.nvg.org/uae/index.html
