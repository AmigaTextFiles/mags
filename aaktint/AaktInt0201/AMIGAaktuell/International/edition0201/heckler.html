The Heckler

The Heckler (by Ralf Driesner)

Breast or drumstick.......?

And again I must note: This world is evil. The audience of a Munich radio station was able to experience live a spectacular happening. In the interests of general wellbeing a new kind of sport was needed. So "Extreme-Microwaving" was invented. Sporting, sporting .... but, confound it, how does it work?

Take a little child, or better, the toy car of a little child.

Furthermore take the sister of that rugrat, who has long suffered the merry-mad-driving sound of exactly that toy car.

The poor sister, whom we all sympathize with, now had this satanic plan to "fry" the toy car in the microwave oven. But all Munich was to learn about this blow for freedom, and thus all of this went "on air".

In all its gory detail the sister related how the nerve-jangling monster metamorphosed into a little shrunken plastic puddle of bleeding colours. At the end of this rite she shared the relief of every toy terrorist when she confirmed that no battery could be connected to that plastic puddle to produce that kind of sound again (to talk about music would have been exaggeration).

"Oh well," says the reader, "a bit less electronics on this planet. But where is the kick?"

Well, cynic that I am, I would have let the kid participate in ceremony. The loud crying of the child at the resolute activity of the sister certainly could have been emphasized.

Before complaints by the German Children's Protection Association pelt down on me serially, I'll try to dare an elegant twist to the Amiga.

One of the biggest problems of recent electronics definitely is the poor protection against noise. Blowers and drives, motors and alarms every day assault our ears. In a capital like Munich this inevitably has an effect upon the psyche. Who hasn't secretly wanted to chuck his neighbour or the computer into the microwave?!

A request to Snoqualmie: Let us hope we will be saved from similar situations at least with our "girlfriend". I really would not like to ....

Live long and prosperous,

yours

Ralf Driesner <global-dries@gmx.de>
