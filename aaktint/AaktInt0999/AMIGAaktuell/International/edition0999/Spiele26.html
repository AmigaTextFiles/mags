26. Emulator news

Do you remember November/December of last year? Fine, because this news is linked to that. It's about the emulators Genecide and Nesticide by Jesse Livengood, that were to run with extremely high speed. Jesse wanted to release these emulators in December '98. But when nobody heard about these two emulators, anybody thought, it was a joke. Now, Richard Sandgren found out the eMail address of Jesse and asked him in this matter. He replied, that he does not program for the Amiga anymore, but he wanted to release Nesticide this week. From Genecide he 'only' offers the sources. So, who wants to continue programming the emulator should report to him. But Genecide seems to be nearly complete.

A new revision of A/NES for CyberGraphX has been published. It can be downloaded from the A/NES webpage <http://www.emuclassics.com/ANES/> and offers improvements in speed and some bugfixes.

Revision 1.01 of Wzonka-Lad has been published. This version allows commercial ROMs again.

A new PowerPC implementation of the MAME emulator can be found in the Aminet. It is based on the lates sources and has not been optimized.

Cloanto has published the Amiga Forever 3.0 CD-ROM. It also includes a later revision of UAE, that supports AGA emulation, now.
