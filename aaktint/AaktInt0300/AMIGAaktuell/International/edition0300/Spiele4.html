Games section

4. Game news from all over the world

  • Elite 3 and Descent 2

Alive Mediasoft officially announced, that Descent 2 and Elite 3 will be released for the Amiga. The port of Descent 2 is nearly ready and they are still working on Elite 3.

  • Simon the Sorcerer 2 News

The 500 pre-orders, which are necessary for a port of Simon the Sorcerer 2, have nearly been reached. On the homepage of Epic marketing some screenshots have been published.

  • Alpha Version from Nightlong

clickBOOM completed the alpha version of Nightlong. Now the game enters the test phase. You can apply for beta-testing, but you have to have a pre-order for becoming a beta-tester. clickBOOM is going to optimze the source and some problems have to be solved (e.g. it seems, that 3 CD-ROMs are not enough for the game).

  • Ali Baba

Ali Baba is a new action adventure from the newcomers of Medici. On their homepage http://www.medici.org.uk/ you can download various things about the game, including a demo version.

  • Demoversion of Foundation DC
This month a playable demo version of Foundation - The Directors Cut has been published. An extended review was included in the last edition of AMIGA aktuell International.
  • Phantasmagoria comes from Alive Mediasoft

Approximately three years ago Phantasmaorgia for the Amiga was announced. Since then there was not much spoken about the successful PC game. Alive Mediasoft confirmed Amiga Flame, that they will release Phantasmaorgia.

  • Digital Images is looking for C++ programmers

Stuart walker of Digital Images announced, that the company is looking for a C++ programmer, who is experienced in GUI programming and AI. In addition Start confirmded, that Digital Images is moving to Los Angeles (California, USA). So the programmer would work there. Well, who of you would like to move to sunny California? Applications to Stuart Walker <djstu@byke.com>.

  • HomeLand Demo version released

Demo version release 1.0 of the Command&Conquer clone HomeLand has been released. The complete game will be released soon and will be published by Alive Mediasoft.

  • Quake 1.09b released by Frank Wille

The new beta version of Quake 1.09 now supports AGA, CyberGraphX, the chunkyppc.library (Screen, Public Screen, WB-window, double-/triple buffering, P96-PiP), keyboard, mouse, joystick as well as TCP/IP and sound (using audio.device). In addition it is possible to play audio CDs. The new quake revision can be downloaded e.g. from the Aminet.

  • Alive Mediasoft is also looking for programmers

Those of you who are interested in working in the Elite 3 or Phantasmaorgia team as a programmer can contact Alive Mediasoft <steve@alive-mediasoft.co.uk> But they are only looking for programmers for temporary help.

  • Tales of Tamar News

Two updates have been released for Tales of Tamar, this month. On http://www.tamar.net they can be downloaded. Tales of Tamar is a multi platform and multi player game using the Internet.

  • Crystal Software brings Pokemon for Amiga

The Pokemon madness seems to come to the Amiga, too: Crystal Software got the rights for porting the Pokemon Nintendo 64 version. According to Crystal Software the game will be ported to PC, Linux and also to AmigaOS. The Amiga-version will be on the same CD-ROM as the PC-version and can be purchased in most shops. For the Amiga version they are still looking for programmers, music artists and graphical designers. If you are interested, contact Crystal Software <vacancies@crystal-software.com>

  • Alive Mediasoft is looking for support for 'The Pulse'

The fre game magazine of Alive Mediasoft 'The Pulse' offers a section with letters to the editor, now. In this section they want to collect the opinions of readers in the future. This is why they ask any Amiga user to participate the discussion. Send your contributions directly to thepulse@alive-mediasoft.co.uk.

  • PowerBall Demo released

Darkage Software released a playable demo version of PowerBall. PowerBall is a simple puzzle game in the style of Megaball. The demo version can be downloaded from the Aminet.

  • Sin for Amiga

Ben Hermanns from Hyperion Software announced in an interview of Amiga Active, that Hyperion Software will port the game 'Sin', which is based on Heretic 2 and Quake 2 engine, to the Amiga. Further details are unknown, yet.

  • PaganGames is looking for programmers

The people of PaganGames are still looking for a talented programmers for their projects.

  • The Heretic Fortress

Steffen Haeuser announced, that his port of the Heretic 2 addons 'The Heretic Fortress' is 99% complete. Screenshots of the Amiga version of Heretic Fortress 4.0 can be watched on members.tripod.com/ppcstation/.
