  • more emulator news:

The A\NES homepage moved again. The new URL is: http://www.emuclassics.com/ANES.

The homepage of SNES9x Ports by Jesper Svennevid also moved. The new URL: http://snes.amiga.tm.

The emulator page Amiga Emulation Centrol is continued after all. David Gonneau joined the team and wants to ensure, that the page is updated more regular. By the way, the URL is: http://www.classicgaming.com/AEC.

The Sega MegaDrive emulator AmiGenerator runs on PPC computers with 35fps, so it is quite fast. The Amiga version can be downloaded on http://www.amidog.com/.

Thanks to the work of Gaelan Griffin, author of Anonymous and SNES9xPlay, the new WarpSNES revision offers sound, now. Due to that the FPS lower from approx. 35 to 27. The new SNES9x PPC revision of Jesper Svennevid is to support sound, too.

A new revision of SNES9x's GUI in under development, so you can set the sound options within the GUI.

The author of CoolNES Frederik 'Flubba' Olsson is looking for people, who like helping him with the work of his emulator. He would like a new GUI, because the old one was to unfriendly to the system and not very nice, either. In addition he's looking for people for various localisations. If you're interested contact Fredrik Olsson <flubba@hem2.passagen.se>.

Jesper Svennevid planns to optimize the 68k version of his SNES9x emulator in assembler. This would enable 68k users to play SNES games with acceptable speed, at last. But he wants feedback, if this is really needed, because it was much work. So write to him, if you want to play SNES games on your 68k Amiga. eMail: pt98jsv@student.hk-r.se.

The emulator page Amiga Emulation Monster seems to have gone. At least it hasn't been actualized since November '98. And at that time they announced a super MegaDrive emulator as well as a NES emulator. Even, if the informations seemed to be trustworthy, it seems to have been a joke, because both emulators were to be released in December '98.

The first emulator appeared for the PC, which is under commercial marketing. It is the Playstation emulator Bleem!, which costs approx. 100 DEM (approx. 51.02 Euros) in the full version. If this won't have no consequences?
