The AMIGA-aktuell-international competition

1. Results of the last competition

67 readers participated the last competition. Compared with the last month this is a small raise. Nevertheless: I'm sure, you can do better, don't you?

But now the solution:

Standard question: What is the name of the AMIGA's Chief Technology Officer (CTO)?


Answer: Of course, the person we were looking for was Dr. Richard (quot;Rick") LeFaivre. 
  
Master question: Name three companies the person we are looking for worked for before being CTO at AMIGA. 
  
Answer: There were a number of companies, that were possible, like Apple, Sun and Silicon Graphics. 
   
Question of opinion: Do you think it is useful, that AMIGA is on headhunt for employees from other companies, that had little or nothing to do with the Amiga? 
  

The results:


Answer Number of Votes 
1 (This has only advantages for the Amiga.) 6 
2 (This has mostly advantages for the Amiga.) 53 
3 (This has mostly disadvantages for the Amiga.) 3 
4 (This has only disadvantages for the Amiga.) 0 
  

Two participants couldn't decide between the numbers 2 and 3 and two more participants didn't answer the question of opinion and therefore forego on the second chance in the competition.

Conclusion:

It's a clear result: Most participants mostly see advantages in hiring managers out of the Amiga range, just a small minority see the disadvantages in the foreground. So, AMIGA seems to have the right strategy, if they also hire AMIGA experienced developers.
