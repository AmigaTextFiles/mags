1. Results of the last competition

Twenty-six readers entered the last competition.

Please consider entering again: Remember, you can win by answering just two questions.

But now, last month's solutions:


Standard Question: In his recent Executive Update Bill McEwen hinted, that at an Amiga show being held before long a »big surprise« will be presented. What is the name of this event?



Answer: The name of the show is »Amiga 2001 - The Gateway Computer 
 Show«, but we accepted parts of the name as correct. 
  
Master Question: In which US-American city will the stated Amiga show be held? 
  
Answer: Correct is »St. Louis«. 
  

Question of opinion: How high do you set the stakes of your expectations for this event with concern to the indications of the AMIGA President?

Possible answers:

1. My expectations are very high.
2. My expectations are not so high.
3. I do not expect very much / nothing at all.


Answer Number of Nominations 
  
1 (My expectations are very high.) 7 
2 (My expectations are not so high.) 12 
3 (I do not expect very much / nothing at all.) 7 
  

Several participants added comments to their answers to the question of opinion:

Krister Skrtic hopes »to learn some good news, especially about the OS3.9 BoingBag. Why aren't there more often smaller updates instead waiting so long?«

Kai Tenbieg writes: »I very deeply hope, that Bill at last will bring the Amiga on to the road of success. I am very curious whether Bill will suffer the same fate as his predecessor, who has been ousted.«

Stephan Kolbe shows little hope: »I expect scarcely anything, for it is my opinion that AMIGA is not interested any more in its present users. For goodness sake, don't let it be like this.«

A similar view appears to be held by Martin R. Elsner: »What kind of surprise can Bill offer right now? At the most the announced extension boards for the Classic Amiga or a new version of the AmigaDE, or a new Amiga ball-pen.«

Rainer M. Herold states: »By and by I don't want to hear words any more but I want to see actions! What I am missing most is a JVM on the Amiga and a browser at least able to do JavaScript!«

Andre Beer is full of expectations: »I have very high expectations and I hope not to be disappointed, because AMIGA Inc. would make itself really untrustworthy after such boastful announcements.«

Tim Moerbe has resigned: »In the meantime I have given up the hope for a new Amiga. For years month by month I have put aside some money for this dream.

This has now become a Honda CBR 600 F - sorry AMIGA, I believe, I gave you a fair chance. - Hello Nils - I have the bike.«

Christian Secker writes: »I hope the news will be super and at last the spirit of Amiga will be back again.«

Gernod Schomberg has not so high expectations, »but the higher hopes!"«

A few days ago the nervously awaited fair came off, and everybody will have their own impressions of the announcements.
